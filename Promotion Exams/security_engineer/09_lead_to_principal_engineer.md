# Promotion Exam: Lead Engineer → Principal Engineer

**Track:** Individual Contributor (IC) — sibling to Lead → Engineering Manager
**Format:** 100 multiple-choice questions
**Time:** 150 minutes
**Passing score:** 89 / 100

**Scope.** Principal Engineer is the senior individual-contributor role above Lead, the peer of the Engineering Manager, and — in this company's ladder — a possible step toward VP of Engineering. A Lead owns technical direction for a team. A Principal owns technical direction across multiple teams or the entire engineering organization: the hardest architecture decisions, the multi-year technical bets, the standards that shape how everyone builds, and the growth of the organization's senior technical talent. The Principal has no direct reports and holds no formal authority over people — their influence travels entirely through trust, judgment, and the quality of their reasoning. That constraint is the defining feature of the role, and most of this exam tests whether the candidate can operate within it.

**Administrator's note.** As with the EM and VP exams in this series, the answer key is intentionally pattern-weighted, with deliberate breaks. Principal-level judgment rarely has one clean answer; the key favors the move that preserves influence, gathers evidence, respects the humans involved, and keeps the long view. The debrief — where the candidate defends their picks and argues where the key is wrong — is the real exam. A candidate who defends a "wrong" answer with sharper reasoning than the key is showing exactly the quality the role demands.

**On the Principal–EM partnership.** This company keeps the IC and management tracks separate, with no crossover. Where a team or org has both a Principal and an EM, they operate as collaborating peers: the EM owns people, process, and delivery; the Principal owns technical strategy, architecture, and the technical growth of the organization. Several questions in this exam test the candidate's understanding of that division and the maturity to work within it.
