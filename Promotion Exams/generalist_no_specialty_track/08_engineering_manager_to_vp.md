# Promotion Exam: Engineering Manager → VP of Engineering

**Format:** 100 multiple-choice questions
**Time:** 180 minutes
**Passing score:** 89 / 100

**Scope.** This is the final exam in the ladder, and the most subjective. The VP of Engineering is responsible for an engineering organization — not a team — and is accountable, with the executive team, for the business outcomes the organization is supposed to produce. The work is org design, strategy, capital allocation, executive partnership, and the slow, deliberate shaping of culture. The questions here are almost entirely scenario-based. The "best" answer is the one a thoughtful VP would defend in front of a board, a CEO, and the engineering org — all three audiences simultaneously.

**Administrator's note.** The passing threshold of 89 is real but not the most important signal. The debrief — where the candidate defends each answer, especially the ones they got "wrong" — is. A candidate who fails three questions but defends each with sharper reasoning than the key is closer to ready than one who memorizes the key. As with the EM exam, the answer key is intentionally pattern-weighted; the goal is to make candidates argue, not match.
